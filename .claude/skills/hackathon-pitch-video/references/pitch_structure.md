# Pitch structure — the narrative beats

A judge-ready hackathon video is a story with a job. Each beat earns a specific thing.
This structure is adapted from a strong real submission narrative (one-line pitch →
problem → MVP delivered → key finding → fit → reproduce → honest limitations → next step),
compressed for a 2-3 minute video.

## The beats (in order)

### 0. Hook — first 10 seconds (earns: attention)
The single most important moment. Judges decide here whether to lean in.
- Open on the **one-line pitch** OR a striking live shot of the thing working.
- No "Hi, we're team X and today we'll be presenting..." That wastes the hook.
- A surprising number, a painful problem stated plainly, or the product doing its
  best trick — pick one.

> Template: *"[Product] is a [category] that [does the one valuable thing] for [who]."*
> Example posture: lead with your sharpest, most defensible claim — the thing only your
> project can say.

### 1. Problem (earns: Theme relevance + why-care)
- One concrete problem, one audience. Not three.
- Why it matters and why it's unsolved / badly solved today.
- Make it felt, not abstract. A real example beats a market-size stat.
- 15-25 seconds.

### 2. Solution + Live demo (earns: Technical Execution + Design/UX — the core)
- This is the spine. Spend the most time here.
- **Show the product doing the job from the problem.** Narrate what's happening and why
  it's the answer — don't tour the UI.
- Demo only what actually runs. A small thing that works beats a big thing that's faked.
- If there's a "wow" capability, time it for the middle so attention stays.
- 60-90 seconds.

### 3. How it works / the clever bit (earns: Innovation + Technical Execution)
- The one technical idea that makes this non-trivial — the thing that isn't a wrapper
  around an off-the-shelf tool.
- Keep it to the insight + one supporting detail. No architecture deep-dives.
- If you have a real result or metric, state it here with its number.
- 20-30 seconds.

### 4. Impact + fit (earns: Theme & Impact)
- Who this helps and how it extends beyond the hackathon.
- Tie explicitly back to the **hackathon's theme/challenge** in the judges' own words.
- 15-20 seconds.

### 5. Honest limitations + next step (earns: credibility, and it genuinely scores)
- Name the real constraints (small dataset, single use-case, rough edges).
- Then the highest-impact next step. This reads as rigor, not weakness — judges reward
  teams that know their own ceiling.
- Optional in very short videos, but it's a differentiator. 10-15 seconds.

### 6. Close (earns: recall + CTA)
- Restate the one-liner. Team name. Where to find it (repo/live link on screen).
- End clean. No long credits roll.
- 5-10 seconds.

## What judges remember

Force the team to fill this in. If they can't, the video isn't focused yet.
- **One sentence:** ____

## Common failure modes (and the fix)

- **Slow open** — 30s of intro/team/agenda before anything happens → cut to the hook.
- **UI tour instead of demo** — clicking through every screen → show one real task end-to-end.
- **Demoing vaporware** — narrating features that don't run → only show what works; say
  what's next for the rest.
- **No clear problem** — solution with no stated pain → add one concrete problem + audience.
- **Burying the finding** — best result mentioned at 2:40 → move it to the hook or right
  after the demo.
- **Over length** — the #1 disqualifier → cut beats 3-5 before you cut the demo.
